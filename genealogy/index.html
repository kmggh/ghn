The Ancestors of John Marion Guyton

by G. Monroe Guyton, Geames W. Guyton, and Ken Guyton

July 1984

This is a preliminary report on our research toward establishing the genealogy of the John Marion Guyton family. We are quite sure that the information for Generations 1 through 4 and Generations 5 through 8 is correct and, although we strongly suspect that John Guyton (Generation 5) is the son of Nathaniel (Generation 4), the linking together of these lines remains to be proven. We hope to add to this information in a future report.

Guytons In France

(Giton, Guiton, Guton, Guyton)

Of French Huguenot lineage, the Guytons were among the French Protestants who left France seeking religious and political freedom, first in England, later in America. In “Maithol’s Directory of the French Nobility” we find that “the Guyton family originated in Normandie, with one branch establishing its residence in Brittany. The family bore the title of count.”

The “Concise History of France” —Guizot-Gustave Masson, records the fact that John Guiton was Burgess of La Rochelle at the time of the seige by Louis XIII, A.D. 1625. On page 353: “The siege of La Rochelle has become famous in history; it lasted thirteen years and the brave Huguenots had to surrender in spite of the heroism of Guiton, the mayor of the town, assisted by the energy of the old Duchess of Robou.”

French history also records the fact that one John W. Guiton was one of the Generals who fought in the Battle of Waterloo[1].

Early Guytons In America

As early as 1669 we find members of the Guyton family living in South Carolina, as in this year the records of the Huguenot Society in Charleston show that Judith Guiton (the widow Royer), was married to Pierre Manigault[1].

GENERATION 3.

John Guyton ( ? –1783) and Mary [2,3,4,5]

Andrea indicates that Mary may have been Mary Whitticar, the daughter of John Whitticar but this is unproven[4].

John Guyton was baptized 19 February 1696, St. Martin at Palace, Norwich, England. He was in Calvert County, Maryland as early as 1732[2] where he was listed in the List of Taxables in Upper Hundred of the Cliffs[5].

He is in “Alyst of the Melitia under the Command Capt. Sutton Isaack,” Upper Hundred of the Cliffs, Calvert County, Maryland, 1748[5].

John and Benjamin Guyton (probably his brother) both moved to Baltimore County, Maryland, probably during the 1750’s, and lived near each other[5].

John obtained a Maryland Land Grant in 1740 and served in the Colonial Wars of Maryland[4].

GENERATION 5.

John Guyton ( ? –1854)[6]

It is not proven that this John is the same John as the son of Nathaniel, mentioned above.

John Guyton’s will was signed 1 July 1854[6].

Philip J. Guyton and Robert H. Guyton are executors of the will of John Guyton. Also one of us (G. Monroe Guyton) remembers accounts of John Marion Guyton (see below) telling about the father of Robert H., “Johnny”, and his slaves “Hen and Sam.” The will mentions three slaves, Hazar, Sam, and Perry.

It is also interesting that Philip J. and Robert H. have exactly the same birth date.

Thus, from this evidence, it is believed that Philip J. and Robert H. are twin brothers and the sons of John.

John does not mention all of his children (or wife) by name in his will so there are possibly more children.

Their children:

Jerney Guyton[6]

William Guyton[6]

Philip J. Guyton (see above) (23 Sep 1811 – 6 Apr 1875)[7]

* Robert H. Guyton (see above)
